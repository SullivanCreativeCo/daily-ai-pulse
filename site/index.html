LINKEDIN DRAFTS

LI . NO. 01
Nvidia gave away a 550 billion parameter AI model this week.

Not because they couldn't sell it. Because the model was never the product.

The free model comes bundled with a free Agent Toolkit. The toolkit is tuned to run fastest on Nvidia hardware. The hardware is where the money is.

Razor and blades. Except the razor is one of the most capable open models on the planet.

It's already working. Cadence, CrowdStrike, Palantir, Siemens, Foxconn. All signed on.

Here's what I keep thinking about as a builder: this pattern shows up everywhere now. Free model. Paid runtime. Free API tier. Paid at scale. Free agent. Paid when it actually does something.

The question worth asking about every free AI tool in your stack: what's the thing they're actually trying to sell?

Because the model is almost never the product anymore.
LI . NO. 02
Anthropic filed its IPO paperwork today.

The company that built its whole identity around responsible AI is about to answer to public markets.

Public markets don't care about mission statements. They care about growth, margin, and ARR.

And that's not a cynical take. It's just math. When you take investor money, you're buying time to prove the thing works. When you go public, the proof has to show up in a spreadsheet.

I don't think Dario Amodei is going to wake up in January and start cutting safety teams. But I do think every internal decision at Anthropic just got a little more visible, a little more measurable.

The IPO race with OpenAI and SpaceX is real now. Whoever files first gets to set the market narrative.

That's not just a finance story. It's a culture story. And the culture of the lab that builds your tools matters more than most people think.
LI . NO. 03
Every meeting your team has today will produce decisions that nobody follows up on.

That's not a criticism. That's just how meetings work. You talk, you decide, you go back to your desk, and the thing that was decided gets buried under 47 Slack messages and two other fires.

ZoomMate launched today with a pretty bold bet: what if the tool where decisions get made was also the tool that executes them?

It connects meeting context to Salesforce, Jira, Slack, ServiceNow. Creates deliverables. Pushes follow-through without you switching apps.

Zoom's CPO called it a system of action. I think that framing is doing a lot of heavy lifting. But the underlying problem is real.

The gap between deciding something and actually getting it done is where most business value disappears.

If you're building internal tools or advising clients on workflows, this is the gap worth closing. It's not glamorous. It doesn't make a good demo. But it removes a weight that every team feels.
