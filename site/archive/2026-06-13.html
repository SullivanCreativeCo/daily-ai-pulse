LI . NO. 02
There's a two-call playbook that converts 30-50% of free AI audits into paid clients.

Corey Ganim dropped it this week and I've been thinking about it since.

The whole framework: find one bottleneck. Prescribe one tool. Upsell the build.

Call one is 15 minutes, pure discovery. You ask five questions. You prescribe nothing. You just listen and name their pain back to them.

Between calls, you figure out where frequency meets friction. That intersection is the bottleneck worth fixing.

Call two, you bring three things. The tool name. The cost. The first step they can take this week if they want to go it alone.

Then you ask the money question: "Do you want me to hand this off, build it with you, or build it for you?"

That's it. That's the whole model.

The reason it works: you deliver value for free first. Trust goes up. Paid work follows.

What are you already doing for yourself or your business that someone else would find useful? Because someone would pay for that 15-minute conversation if it found the one thing dragging their operation down.
LI . NO. 03
The document isn't the right container for most knowledge work. Never really was.

OpenAI shipped a feature in Codex this week that lets you publish what you're building as a live website in one step. No Vercel, no database, no wiring anything together.

It feels like a small release. It's not.

The bigger idea: a URL is a better artifact than a PDF for almost everything knowledge workers actually ship. Proposals. Strategy memos. Onboarding guides. Training materials. The document format was a constraint we inherited, not a choice we made.

PDFs go stale the second you send them. Links stay current. Docs force a linear path. Sites let different readers jump to the part that matters to them. And when you update the site, everyone who has the link gets the update automatically.

We were stuck on documents because websites used to require a whole team. AI erased that cost.

So the honest question now: what are you still packaging as a static file that would actually work better as a living link?

I don't have a clean answer yet for everything I ship. But I'm rethinking a few things.
