LINKEDIN DRAFTS

LI . NO. 01
78% of customers hire the first company that gives them a quote.

Not the best company. Not the cheapest.

The fastest.

Chris Koerner opened his latest video with that stat and I haven't stopped thinking about it. He was talking about garage door repair businesses, but this applies to pretty much any service business that's still calling people back during business hours.

He built an AI voice agent for it live on camera. 42 minutes. No code. Just Lovable and GoHighLevel and a clear idea of what problem he was solving.

That's the part I keep coming back to. He didn't start with 'what can AI do?' He started with 'what does this person lose when they don't respond fast enough?'

Get your foot in the door. Sell them more stuff. He literally said that.

The product is the speed. The recurring revenue is proof it works.

What are you already doing for yourself that a business owner would pay $300 a month not to think about?
LI . NO. 02
Anthropic just filed for an IPO at a reported $965 billion valuation.

That's the company that opened its own website with 'Anthropic is an AI safety company.' The company that said it might be building one of the most dangerous technologies in history.

And they meant it. I actually believe they meant it.

But here's where it gets interesting. Public companies answer to shareholders every 90 days. Safety research doesn't move on a 90-day cadence. Cutting safety teams to hit a number is how you get the story nobody wants to write.

OpenAI already crossed this bridge when they restructured. Now Anthropic joins the race.

I don't think this is a gotcha moment. Some of the best safety work will happen inside companies with real resources. Underfunded nonprofits don't win arms races.

But the pressure to ship faster, price higher, and explain every dollar to investors is a new kind of pressure. And that tension is worth watching closely.

What does 'responsible development' mean in an S-1?
LI . NO. 03
We just had the most agent-focused week in AI history and I don't think it's getting framed that way.

Nvidia shipped a 500-billion-parameter model built specifically for agent workflows. Their CEO called agents 'the next business computing pattern after mobile and cloud.'

Asana relaunched as an operating system for human-agent teams.

TownAI raised $55 million to build an AI that learns you over time.

Hermes shipped a desktop app so anyone can run a self-improving agent without touching a terminal.

Microsoft released a reasoning model and an always-on background agent for office work.

That's five companies in five days all betting on the same shift.

I keep thinking about what happened between 2008 and 2012. The mobile app infrastructure got built. The people who figured out 'what do people actually want to do on a phone?' made the money.

The infrastructure for agents is getting built right now. The question still up for grabs: what do people actually want agents to do?

That question isn't answered yet. And I think that's kind of the whole ballgame right now.

AI launches & news

  • Nvidia's Agent Infrastructure Bet — Computex 2026 Three announcements, one thesis: agents are the primary computing workload now. Nemotron 3 Ultra is a 500B-parameter open-weights MoE model built for tool use and long-horizon agent tasks. Vera Rubin, Nvidia's CPU+GPU pairing, is in full production with OpenAI and Anthropic already taking delivery; it's the first Nvidia data center chip designed around the CPU because orchestration beats raw matrix math for agentic workloads. The RTX Spark, a prosumer-grade CPU with 1 petaflop of AI compute and 128GB unified memory, hits Windows laptops this fall as Nvidia's direct answer to Apple M-series dominance in local inference. https://www.abhs.in/blog/nvidia-nemotron-3-ultra-500b-moe-agent-toolkit-computex-2026

"GPU-powered chatbots, that era is ending. Agents are the new workload. They will run everywhere from the data center to the edge." — Carabriski, Nvidia VP for Gen AI Software

  • Microsoft Build 2026: MAI-Thinking-1, Frontier Tuning, Scout — The Decoder Microsoft shipped its first in-house reasoning model: MAI-Thinking-1, a 1-trillion-parameter MoE (35B active, 128K context) that benchmarks near Deepseek V3.2. More interesting for builders is "Frontier Tuning," an RL-based method that lets companies adapt models to their own workflows at roughly one-tenth the cost of GPT-5.4 while matching its performance. Scout is Microsoft's always-on background agent for scheduling and meeting prep, paired with new local developer hardware and an OS rebuilt for agent workloads. https://the-decoder.com/build-2026-microsoft-tops-google-in-image-generation-while-playing-catch-up-on-reasoning/

  • Gemma 4 12B + Grok Imagine 1.5 — Google DeepMind / xAI Google shipped an encoder-free multimodal model designed to run locally on a 16GB VRAM laptop. It's the first mid-sized Gemma model with native audio input, and the encoder-free architecture reduces multimodal latency by feeding vision and audio directly into the LLM backbone. Separately, xAI released Grok Imagine 1.5 to API and standard subscribers; Musk demoed it with a 40-second Iliad trailer that pulled 18.4 million views in under 24 hours and prompted a public "Want full movie?" The quality gap between "impressive for AI" and "just impressive" is visibly closing. https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/developers-tools/introducing-gemma-4-12b/

Worth noting: Anthropic's own October white paper said sovereign wealth funds "could acquire positions in AI-related assets in scenarios where the AI sector captures an outsized share of economic wealth."   • Bernie Sanders Introduces AI Sovereign Wealth Fund Act — AI Daily Brief Sanders will introduce legislation requiring a one-time 50% stock tax on major AI labs, with shares going into a federal fund that gives the government board control and makes direct dividend payments to US citizens. It likely doesn't pass, but the political discourse has shifted from "regulate AI" to "own AI." The tell: OpenAI's own industrial policy white paper called for a public wealth fund, and Anthropic wrote a similar case last fall. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=L2ZJ5HITV48

"Since AI is built on the collective knowledge of humanity, the wealth it generates must benefit humanity." — Bernie Sanders

  • Meta Business Agent Goes Global on WhatsApp — TechCrunch After nearly two years of testing in India and Mexico, Meta's customer support AI is now live globally inside WhatsApp Business and Instagram DMs. It handles support, product recommendations, appointment booking, and lead qualification, routing to humans when needed. Daily briefings of overnight chats are in testing. With 3B+ WhatsApp users, this is likely the largest small-business AI deployment in history, shipping quietly enough that most people missed it. https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/03/metas-ai-agent-for-whatsapp-business-is-now-available-globally/

  • Hermes Desktop — Nous Research / Decrypt Nous Research shipped an official desktop app for Hermes Agent, their open-source self-improving agent. The core mechanic: when Hermes solves a new problem, it saves the approach as a reusable skill document and applies it next time without being re-explained. Previously terminal-only with community-built workarounds; now it's download and run. That crossing from "developer project" to "official UI" is the moment tools become generally usable. https://decrypt.co/369952/hermes-ai-agent-official-app-terminal Why this matters for thought leaders: Hermes is the best live demo of the compounding AI argument. An agent that builds its own skill memory is a categorically different product than a chat interface. Post angle: the difference between AI that resets every session and AI that accumulates.

The Work Graph data model does the contextual heavy lifting here. https://siliconangle.com/2026/06/04/asana-launches-ai-powered-products-help-organizations-manage-human-agent-work/ Why this matters for thought leaders: The PM layer for mixed human-agent teams is an emerging product category.
