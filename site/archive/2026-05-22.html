LINKEDIN DRAFTS

LI . NO. 01
The "you need a big model for that" excuse just got a lot harder to make.

Microsoft shipped MagenticLite this week. An agent framework built specifically for small language models.

Their Fara1.5 model is 9 billion parameters. It handles browser tasks, file navigation, form filling. And it nearly doubles the web navigation performance of the model before it.

That's not a proof of concept. That's a working agentic stack that runs at a fraction of frontier compute costs.

Here's why this matters in practice: a lot of businesses have been sitting on AI agent ideas because the API costs felt like a variable they couldn't control. If capable agents can run on small models, that math changes.

The question becomes less "can we afford to run this" and more "what do we want to hand off first."

What's the most repetitive task in your workflow right now that a small model could probably handle?
LI . NO. 02
A million-token context window changes what "one model" actually means.

Qwen3.7 dropped at Alibaba's Cloud Summit this week. Reasoning built in. 1M tokens of context. Single model.

For reference: a million tokens is roughly 750,000 words. Your entire company's documentation. Your whole codebase. A year of email threads. In one shot.

What changes? You stop architecting workarounds for memory limits. The retrieval layer gets a lot simpler when the model can just hold the whole thing.

The interesting tell: two preview versions showed up quietly on an AI leaderboard before any official announcement. The community found it before the press release did.

That's the current pace. Models are shipping before the marketing catches up.

The question worth sitting with: if context stopped being the constraint, what would you actually try?
LI . NO. 03
Meta built an app that makes your Facebook groups searchable by a chatbot.

It's called Forum. iOS. You browse groups, post to them, ask questions, and a chatbot pulls answers from group content.

On the surface: another Meta product trying to make Facebook feel relevant again.

Under the surface: this is what happens when AI gets an interface into community-generated knowledge. The post someone made in a niche group three years ago becomes a retrievable answer. The forum thread nobody remembers writing is now an AI source.

Communities have been creating knowledge for years with no real way to surface it. That's the problem this mechanic solves, if they can pull it off.

Worth noticing: every comment you've left in a Facebook group is now a potential answer source for someone else's question. That content didn't go anywhere. It just got a new job.

What communities are you part of that are sitting on more useful knowledge than people realize?

Enterprise AI moves

  • Kore.ai Launches Artemis Agent Platform — Morningstar / Business Wire Kore.ai's Artemis platform promises to cut enterprise multiagent deployment from months to days, launching initially on Microsoft Azure. The centerpiece is Agent Blueprint Language (ABL), a compiled declarative language that standardizes how AI agents are defined, validated, and governed before they ever touch production. Six built-in orchestration patterns (including supervisor, delegation, and handoff) are baked in. This is the governance and operational layer story, not raw capability. That's notable because governance is still the actual blocker in most large org deployments, not model quality. https://www.morningstar.com/news/business-wire/20260521284219/koreai-launches-artemis-the-new-generation-of-the-koreai-agent-platform-for-building-governing-and-optimizing-enterprise-ai

New harnesses & tools

  • MagenticLite: Microsoft's Agentic Stack Built for Small Language Models — Digg / Microsoft AI Frontiers Microsoft AI Frontiers shipped MagenticLite, a full agentic framework purpose-built for small models. It ships with two new models: Fara1.5 (9B), a new SOTA small computer-use model that nearly doubles web navigation performance over its Fara-7B predecessor, and MagenticBrain (14B), an orchestrator that plans, codes, and delegates. The stack covers browser tasks, local file system operations, GUI interaction, and form-filling. GitHub is already live. Why this matters for thought leaders: On-device and low-compute agentic AI is now a shipped, benchmarked reality. The conversation with clients shifts from "can we afford AI agents" to "which tasks are right for a small model vs. a frontier one." That's a much more useful conversation to be leading. https://digg.com/ai/i6pkafxj
