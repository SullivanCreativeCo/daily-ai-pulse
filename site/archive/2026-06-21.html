You don't have to write the automation.

You just have to do the task once, in front of it.

OpenAI shipped Record & Replay in Codex this week. You perform a task. Codex watches. Then it converts your demonstration into something it can run on demand, on its own.

No scripting. No prompt engineering. No workflow builder. Show it once and you're done.

Researchers have been trying to build this since the 1980s. It kept breaking at the same step: getting a system to infer a reusable pattern from a single example. Rule-based logic couldn't do it reliably. What changed is routing that step through a language model instead.

The part I keep coming back to: this isn't just for developers. Anyone with a task they hate doing and have to repeat has a use for this.

The barrier to automation just dropped in a real way.
