Greg Isenberg just built three working businesses in 30 minutes.

A dead domain flipper. A restaurant liquidation broker. A hiring-signal cold outreach machine.

The tool is Genspark Claw. Costs $25 a month. Runs Claude Sonnet 4.6 in the cloud. When a bug shipped HTML entities into the drafts, he just said "strip the HTML entities" and it fixed itself.

Here's the framework he's using:

Find a messy public feed. Identify a mispriced asset. Look for a trigger event. Name an obvious buyer with money. Pick a liquidity point.

That's it.

He's not chasing billion-dollar ideas. He's building boring, cash-flowing businesses that make $200 to $1,500 a day.

The episode is 30 minutes and it's the best "ship something this month" content anyone's released in Q2.

Anthropic added dreaming to Claude agents on May 6.

Harvey's legal AI team turned it on. Didn't change their prompts. Didn't swap models.

Task completion rates jumped 6x.

Dreaming is a scheduled background process. The agent reviews past sessions. Extracts patterns. Restructures memory to keep signal quality high. It surfaces insights the agent missed the first time.

No human retraining. No manual tuning.

This is the unlock everyone's been waiting for. Agents that improve over time without you babysitting them.

Anthropic also shipped outcomes grading and multiagent orchestration in the same release. But dreaming is the one that changes the math.

Because here's what just became possible:

You build an agent. You let it run for 90 days. It learns from its own mistakes while you're asleep. By month four, it's better at your process than you are at explaining your process.

That's not a demo. That's production infrastructure.

Would someone pay for that? I think someone would pay for that.
