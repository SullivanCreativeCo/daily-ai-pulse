LINKEDIN DRAFTS

LI . NO. 01
ChatGPT can see your bank account now.

Not a beta. Not a demo. Real accounts. Real spending. Real investment balances.

OpenAI launched personal finance integration for Pro users this week. Connect through Plaid. Ask it if you can afford a house in five years. Ask it which subscriptions you forgot about.

It runs on GPT-5.5 Thinking by default.

Here's the thing nobody's saying out loud.

Most people won't use it.

Not because it doesn't work. Because they don't trust it with their money yet.

The gap between what AI can do and what people will let it do is the real signal. Not the feature. The trust lag.

That gap closes slower than you think. And it closes faster in some categories than others.

Finance is one of the slow ones.

So the question isn't "is this useful?" It obviously is. The question is "how long until normal people connect their Chase account to ChatGPT without a second thought?"

That's the adoption curve that matters.
LI . NO. 02
NVIDIA just made minute-long video generation work on a single GPU.

SANA-WM. 2.6B parameters. 60-second clips at 720p in 34 seconds on an RTX 5090.

Open-source. Apache 2.0 license. Available now on GitHub.

Every other competitive platform requires multi-GPU inference or sacrifices resolution to stay under compute budgets. NVIDIA trained this in 18.5 days on 64 H100s using just 212,975 public video clips.

The breakthrough is memory. Hybrid Gated DeltaNet + softmax attention keeps the recurrent state constant no matter how long the video runs. That's the unlock.

So here's what that means.

Video generation just stopped being a cloud product.

You don't need to upload raw footage to someone else's server. You don't need to wait in a render queue. You don't need to pay per minute.

You run it locally. On hardware you already own.

That changes the math for creators, businesses, and anyone who doesn't want their content sitting on a third-party platform before it's published.

The move from cloud-only to local-capable is always the moment when early adopters become normal users.

We just hit that moment for long-form video generation.
LI . NO. 03
Greg Brockman is consolidating OpenAI's products into a single platform.

ChatGPT. Codex. The API. One core product team. One unified experience.

He sent a staff memo this week. "We're consolidating our product efforts to execute with maximum focus toward the agentic future, to win across both consumer and enterprise."

That's not strategy speak. That's a forcing function.

Sam Altman called code red at the end of last year. OpenAI halted side projects. Sora. OpenAI for Science. Anything that wasn't core.

Now Brockman is merging everything that is core.

So what does "maximum focus toward the agentic future" actually mean?

It means OpenAI isn't building a portfolio of AI tools anymore. They're building one platform where agents live, work, and get deployed.

Consumer agents. Enterprise agents. Developer agents. All running on the same rails.

The question for everyone else is simple.

Are you building tools that plug into that platform? Or are you building tools that compete with it?

Because the companies that win in the next 24 months are the ones that picked the right answer to that question six months ago.

New harnesses & tools

  • Vercel Labs ships Zero: a systems language designed for AI agents — MarkTechPost Vercel released Zero, an experimental systems programming language built so AI agents can read, repair, and ship native programs. It sits in the same design space as C or Rust (compiles to native, explicit memory control, low-level targets), but the compiler output and toolchain were designed from day one to be consumed by agents, not humans. The core problem it solves: agents don't parse unstructured compiler errors well. Zero gives them structured data, predictable tokens, stable codes, and machine-parseable repair hints. This is the first major attempt at building a language where the compiler is an agent interface, not a developer interface. Why this matters for thought leaders: The next generation of developer tools won't just be AI-assisted—they'll be agent-first. Zero is the earliest public signal of what that looks like. https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/05/17/vercel-labs-introduces-zero-a-systems-programming-language-designed-so-ai-agents-can-read-repair-and-ship-native-programs/
