LI . NO. 01
Cursor dropped Composer 2.5 yesterday.

Same pretrain base as 2.0. Scaled significantly on RL and continual pretraining. More intelligent. Better at sustained work. More reliable at following complex instructions.

They doubled usage limits for a week.

Elon said it's partially trained on Colossus 2. Sasha Rush from the Cursor team confirmed they're exploring text feedback and OPSD. Clem from Hugging Face called out the bigger play: "ultimately all serious companies in AI will want to train models themselves."

The move is simple.

If you want to own the layer, you train your own weights.

Cursor's doing it. Google just open-sourced Gemma 4 with four model sizes. GitHub shipped Spaces API to GA and made GPT-5.3-Codex the new base model.

The companies shipping executable tools today are the ones defining the infrastructure tomorrow.

So the question isn't "should I care about model training?"

The question is "what layer am I building on and who owns it?"
LI . NO. 02
Zenlytic just shipped Zoë Self-Learning.

It's an AI data analyst that onboards itself.

Connects to your data warehouse. Identifies the relevant tables. Builds the semantic layer in the background. Produces trusted answers with citations. No data engineering work required.

They claim data to decisions in 59 minutes or less.

For context: most enterprise AI analytics requires 6-month data model buildouts, complex YAML configurations, and tribal knowledge buried in legacy dashboards. Zoë eliminates that setup tax.

Inference Room shipped something similar this week called Tack. It's an agent-native storage layer. No API keys to provision. Went straight to GA with no waitlist. They're promising at least one new AI Agent product every month.

The pattern is the same.

The agent that onboards itself is the wedge that kills the 6-month enterprise sales cycle.

If it works, the bottleneck moves from "convince procurement" to "convince your CFO you don't need a data team."

Would someone pay to skip six months of setup? I think someone would pay to skip six months of setup.
