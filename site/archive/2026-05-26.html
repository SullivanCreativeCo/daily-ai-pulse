Today's headline

Agents aren't becoming fully autonomous. They're becoming coordination layers. The real shift is the "human sandwich"—humans framing problems and judging output, AI executing in the middle—and the entire infrastructure stack is reorganizing around that loop rather than trying to eliminate it.

LINKEDIN DRAFTS

LI . NO. 01
Agents aren't replacing you.

They're making you more important.

Dan Shipper from Every calls it the human sandwich. You frame the problem. AI collapses it into drafts, code, analysis. You come back, judge if it's good, extend the work. You do it again.

That loop is where all the leverage lives right now.

For months everyone talked about agents like they'd work 24/7 while you sleep. Turns out the teams shipping the best work are the ones still in the loop. Constantly steering. Constantly deciding.

The move isn't full autonomy. It's coordination.
LI . NO. 02
Growth is a default.

Staying small is a strategy.

Andre Janusz runs Logan House Coffee Roasters. He had every chance to scale. New locations. New wholesale accounts. Market expansion. Instead he asks one question on every decision: Will this bring me closer to my customers, or create distance?

He calls it friction.

Most founders see friction as a problem to solve. Andre sees it as a signal. If a new location puts something between you and the people you built for, you say no. That's a choice not many can afford to make.

But the ones who do? They build something people actually remember.
LI . NO. 03

They were about coordination.

Grok V9. Qwen 3.7. Gemma 4. You could read the parameter counts and benchmarks. But the real story is different.

Reasonix saves $50 per session by caching shared context better. DevRev released shared memory so teams don't start from scratch every session. Google shipped Agent Executor so agents can actually coordinate at scale. CyOps has AI critiquing AI's code.

The math changed. Bigger parameters matter less than smarter loops between humans, AI, and other AIs.

Coordination is the new frontier.

A direct response to token shortage constraints across the industry. The narrative shift is unmistakable—from "building agents" to "operating agents at scale." This is infrastructure, not a toy. https://www.computerworld.com/article/4176809/google-adds-open-source-agent-executor-to-support-ai-agents-in-production-3.html

  • DevRev Computer AI (shared memory) — DevRev Latest version adds shared context at three levels: individual, team, organizational. Sessions now build on previous work instead of cold-starting. This is the "human sandwich" in infrastructure form—agents need to remember what humans decided last week. https://cmotech.in/story/devrev-adds-shared-memory-to-enterprise-computer-ai

New harnesses & tools

  • Reasonix (DeepSeek-native terminal agent) — Winbuzzer Open-source CLI coding agent built around DeepSeek's prefix caching. Claims ~$12 per session vs. ~$61 without caching on long-running tasks. The value isn't the model. It's the caching layer. For every company running agents continuously, this is the math that changes the unit economics. https://winbuzzer.com/2026/05/25/reasonix-launches-deepseek-native-terminal-coding-agent-xcxwbn/ Why this matters for thought leaders: Cost arbitrage in agents has moved from "which model" to "how do you cache context." The winners this year won't be companies with the biggest models. They'll be the ones who optimized the feedback loop.

"Lowering the running cost of AI agents."

  • CyOps (autonomous coding + verification) — Cysic Browser-based platform that uses two independent AI models: one writes code, the second adversarially critiques and validates it. Solves a real problem—solo AI generation has no peer review. This is the "human sandwich" with AI on both sides of the loop. https://markets.businessinsider.com/news/currencies/cysic-launches-cyops-ai-platform-for-autonomous-coding-and-code-verification-1036192230 Why this matters for thought leaders: Verification is becoming a distinct product category. The future isn't "AI writes, human reviews." It's "AI writes, AI reviews, human judges." That's a different skill set entirely.

  • Reallusion AI Studio + Seedance 2.0 — Reallusion 3D precision control layer (spatial structure via iClone) paired with ByteDance's image generation (Seedance 2.0). The thesis: generative AI has always lacked precision. By anchoring generation to 3D geometry, artists retain control while AIs handle visual richness. Partnership between 3D infrastructure and generative models. https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/3d-precision-control-meets-cinematic-ai-reallusion-unveils-ai-studio-in-creative-alliance-with-bytedances-seedance-2-0--302781097.html Why this matters for thought leaders: The "precision + generation" hybrid is the pattern of 2026. Every domain looking to ship fast will need both a structural layer (3D, JSON, code) and a generative layer. Mixing them is where the craft happens.

Content angles for you

  1. The human sandwich is the unit of work now. Not "AI replaces the human." The question is how tight can you make the loop: human intention → AI execution → human judgment → repeat. That feedback density is where all the leverage lives. Write about why tools like Claude Code and Codex matter more than bigger models.

  2. Intentional smallness as competitive moat. Andre's question—"Will this bring us closer or create distance?"—applies to every growth decision in an agent economy. Build the piece on why founders who stay small by design are choosing better than those who scale by default.

  3. Coordination layers beat capability layers. This week's releases (Reasonix, DevRev, Google Agent Executor, CyOps) all solve the same problem: how do agents talk to each other, how do humans stay in control, how do you avoid cold-starting? That's more valuable than another benchmark win.
