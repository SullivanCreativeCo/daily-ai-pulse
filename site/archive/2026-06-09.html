LINKEDIN DRAFTS

LI . NO. 01
Most teams aren't AI native.
They're using AI the same way they used to copy-paste Google searches.
That's not the same thing.

Theo Tabah broke down what it actually means on Greg Isenberg's podcast today. Three layers. That's it.

People handle the bookends. Strategy, taste, judgment. The stuff that still needs a human attached to it.

Agents handle the middle. Execution, research, output, QA. They run like a new hire once you give them a clear goal, the right skills, and enough context.

Context is the brain. Folders of markdown files agents can read, write to, and pull from. Every client call, every decision, every lesson feeds back in. The company literally gets smarter over time.

The demo was wild. A full branded proposal microsite for a Spotify prospect, with personal details from past calls woven in, ready in under four minutes.

Speed isn't the product. The signal is.

What are you already building that could become the context layer for your whole operation?
LI . NO. 02
The head of Claude Code runs five AI sessions in his terminal at the same time.
Five to ten more open in the browser.
Not because he's a power user. Because that's the workflow now.

Boris Cherny at Anthropic described it as air traffic control. You're not writing code anymore. You're directing agents that write code, QA code, and document code while you're already three steps ahead.

The mental shift is real. You stop being the one who does the work. You become the one who defines it, checks it, and ships it.

Same business card. Different job.

Most people I talk to are still treating AI like fancy autocomplete. The ones moving fastest are running parallel sessions like they've got a whole team behind them.

You don't need Boris's setup to start. You just need to stop doing one task at a time and start assigning them.

What would you hand off to a new assistant on day one?

That's your starting point.
LI . NO. 03
Apple and Google built AI models together.
Not a partnership announcement. Actual co-built foundation models shipping in iOS this year.

That sentence should feel a little weird if you remember watching them fight over default search deals for the last decade.

Apple dropped five new foundation models today. Two run fully on-device, one at 20 billion parameters. No data leaves your phone. New Siri runs on it. Built from the ground up with Google.

Here's what I keep thinking about: the two companies most committed to user privacy just decided co-building was worth more than staying competitors in the model race.

That's not just a technical decision. That's a market bet.

If the next wave of on-device AI is built around privacy as the actual product, not just the disclaimer at the bottom of a terms page, the competitive map shifts. OpenAI and Anthropic are fighting over API calls. Apple and Google just built the layer underneath.

Which version of AI wins when users start actually caring where their data goes?

AI launches & news

  • Apple Foundation Models 3 (AFM 3) — Apple Machine Learning Research Five models total: two run fully on-device (a 3B dense model and a new 20B sparse multimodal model), the rest on Private Cloud Compute. The 20B model handles expressive voice, high-accuracy dictation, and app-level intelligence without data leaving the device. Co-built with Google from the ground up. This isn't an incremental Siri patch; it's a ground-up architectural rebuild with privacy as the design constraint, not the afterthought. https://machinelearning.apple.com/research/introducing-third-generation-of-apple-foundation-models

  • NVIDIA Cosmos 3 — ENGtechnica Launched at GTC Taipei, Cosmos 3 is an open omnimodel for physical AI combining vision reasoning, world generation, and action prediction in one system. Targets robots, autonomous vehicles, and vision AI, with physics-based accuracy. NVIDIA also launched the Cosmos Coalition, with Runway, Black Forest Labs, and Skild AI as members. The promise: cut physical AI training cycles from months to days. https://engtechnica.com/nvidia-launches-cosmos-3-open-model-for-physical-ai/

"The big bang of physical AI is just around the corner thanks to breakthroughs in multimodal reasoning language, vision and world models."

  • Xiaomi MiMo-V2.5-Pro-UltraSpeed — MarkTechPost Xiaomi hit 1,000+ tokens per second on a 1-trillion-parameter MoE model using a single commodity 8-GPU node. The trick is a three-layer co-design: FP4 quantization, restructured attention, and serving-system changes working together. If inference speed becomes the consumer benchmark that matters, running frontier-scale models on non-custom silicon just changed the cost math. https://www.marktechpost.com/2026/06/08/xiaomi-mimo-and-tilert-push-a-1-trillion-parameter-model-past-1000-tokens-per-second-on-commodity-gpus/
