Apple rebuilt Siri from the ground up at WWDC 2026 and shipped it powered by Google Gemini. Not Apple Intelligence, not a proprietary model built in-house. Their biggest rival's stack. LINKEDIN DRAFTS

LI . NO. 01
Apple just shipped the new Siri. The engine running it is Google Gemini.

Not Apple Intelligence. Not a proprietary model they spent years building in-house. Their biggest rival's model.

Think about what that tells you.

Apple has more resources than almost any company on earth. A massive ML research team. The hardware advantage they've spent decades building. The privacy-first brand positioning they've staked their identity on. And they looked at all of that and decided the model layer wasn't where they were going to compete.

They built in the application layer instead.

If that's Apple's call, it's worth asking what it means for everyone else. The race was never about who built the best model. It was always about who built the best experience on top of one.

That's still wide open.

What are you building on top of?
LI . NO. 02
Your AI agent now has a brokerage account and a credit card.

Not a concept. Not a pilot program. Live, this week.

Robinhood launched agentic trading Monday. You create an isolated bot account, set your spending limits, connect your AI agent through their MCP server, and fund it. The agent gets its own credit card number. You get a live notification every time it makes a trade.

MetaMask launched their AI Agent Wallet the same week. 200 early access spots. Trades across 25 blockchains, with simulations and threat scanning on every transaction.

Both of them on the same day.

Six months ago this was whitepaper territory. Now it's something you can sign up for.

The guardrails are real and they matter. But the infrastructure being real changes the conversation.

Agents aren't coming for finance. They're already in there, running trades.
LI . NO. 03
There are two groups of AI users now. The gap between them is getting wider, not stabilizing.

The AI Daily Brief nailed the inflection point: late 2025, when coding tools stopped being just for engineers. Every knowledge worker could suddenly use code and custom apps to solve their own problems. That was the moment.

The people who caught it moved to agent loops. Running automated workflows, building bespoke tools, spending time on decisions instead of tasks.

The people who didn't catch it are still using chat the same way they were 18 months ago.

OpenAI is reacting to this. The ChatGPT overhaul coming in the next few weeks is specifically designed to push casual users toward coding, agents, and integrations. The app is being redesigned around the gap.

The tools are changing to close the distance. The question is what you do before they get there.

  • MiniMax M3 Makes 1M Context Viable With Sparse Attention | Awesome Agents Million-token context windows existed before M3. The problem was cost: standard attention scales quadratically with sequence length, making 1M-token requests prohibitively expensive at real usage volumes. MiniMax's custom sparse attention architecture breaks that curve, pricing input at $0.60/M tokens (halved at launch), with weights hitting Hugging Face around June 11. This is the story where long-context becomes an actual deployment decision rather than a benchmark flex. https://awesomeagents.ai/news/minimax-m3-sparse-attention-1m-context/

"Standard full attention has to consider every position in the context window when computing each output token. At 1M tokens that means attending to one million key-value pairs for every produced token."
