  • Gemini 3.5 Flash: Google's new agentic backbone — Google Blog Google shipped Gemini 3.5 Flash globally today—4x faster than comparable frontier models, now the default for Gemini app and AI Mode in Search. Available today to AI Plus, Pro, and Ultra subscribers. Omni sits alongside 3.5 Flash as part of Google's two-model strategy: Flash for speed and action, Omni for rich multimodal creation. The framing that matters: OpenAI has Sora, Anthropic is training video models, and Google just made sure it's not sitting out the video generation arms race. https://www.cnbc.com/2026/05/19/google-ai-ultra-gemini-spark-omni.html

  • H2O.ai's tabH2O: predictions from spreadsheets without training — The Next Web H2O.ai launched tabH2O at Dell Technologies World 2026—a foundation model for tabular data that delivers predictions from CSVs via a single API call. No training runs, no feature engineering, no persistent storage. It uses in-context learning to read labeled data and return classification, regression, and time-series predictions in seconds. Pre-integrated with Dell AI Factory and NVIDIA, supports air-gapped deployment. Why this matters for thought leaders: The data science workflow just collapsed from weeks to seconds. If you've been selling "we'll train a custom model," the product you're selling might not exist in six months. https://thenextweb.com/news/h2o-ai-launches-tabh2o-a-foundation-model-that-makes-predictions-from-tabular-data-without-any-training

  • NVIDIA Nemotron-Labs-Diffusion: 6x tokens per forward pass — MarkTechPost NVIDIA released Nemotron-Labs-Diffusion, a tri-mode language model family (3B, 8B, 14B parameters) that supports autoregressive decoding, diffusion-based parallel decoding, and self-speculation. Claims 6x more tokens per forward pass than Qwen3-8B. Includes base, instruct, and vision-language variants. The technical crowd will care about the architecture.
