LI . NO. 01
The best AI acquisition method in 2026 is almost boring.

Corey Ganim walked through seven ways to land AI clients this week. Meetups. Door knocking. LinkedIn outreach. Agency partnerships. You know what they all have in common? A free 15-minute assessment.

Not free access to a tool. Not a demo video. A live conversation where you actually look at their broken process and say, "Here's one thing that would fix it."

One person knocked on 30 doors. Got five appointments. Two became paying clients.

Another hosted a meetup in a realtor's office, followed up within 24 hours. Seven qualified leads in two hours of work.

The reason this works: you're not selling them AI. You're removing a pain that's costing them time every single week. Once they see removal is possible, they'll ask how much.

Most people skip this. They want to build the perfect product first. Or sell at scale. But the people winning right now are the ones doing it in person, on foot, in their own town.

That's not a marketing hack. That's just how humans make decisions when it matters.
LI . NO. 02
The model releases this week are getting harder to tell apart.

Alibaba dropped Qwen3.7-Max. NVIDIA shipped Nemotron-3-Ultra. Google released Gemma 4 with quantization-aware training. They're all good. They're all fast. They all have a million token context.

And honestly? Most teams don't feel the difference.

What actually moved the needle this week was Zoom shipping ZoomMate and Microsoft shipping Scout. Two products that say the same thing: stop switching between tools.

You're in a meeting. Decision gets made. Now what? Scout reads the room, drafts an email, files a ticket, clears a calendar while you're already onto the next thing.

That's the product that matters. Not the model underneath. The coordination layer.

Models are becoming like cloud servers. You need them. They matter at scale. But no one gets excited about them at a coffee shop anymore.

The companies winning are the ones building products on top of models, not the models themselves.

So here's the question: what workflow in your business would change if you had something that just handled the handoff?
LI . NO. 03
The biggest opportunity in AI right now is unsexy.

The people making real money from AI aren't building frontier models. They're not launching the next ChatGPT. They're knocking on doors in their own town.

Corey Ganim's frame is simple: a free 15-minute AI assessment. You look at a local business, find the one thing slowing them down, show them how to fix it. Then you ask if they want to fix the other four things.

What I like about this: it's immediate. It's real. It's repeatable.

You don't need perfect tools. You don't need a massive brand. You need to be in the room with someone and say, "I see what's broken. I know how to unbreak it."

That's how every service business starts. That's how it compounds. One person tells another. One town becomes two towns. One product becomes ten.

This is how Intercom got their first hundred customers. How HubSpot built a moat. Not with hype. With audits.

So if you're sitting on an idea of "I could help people use AI better," here's your move: pick a town. Pick a business type. Show up. Do the audit for free. See what happens.

Baby bird has gotta jump outta the nest.
