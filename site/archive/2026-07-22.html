AI launches & news

  • Kimi K3 - Moonshot AI Moonshot shipped a 2.8 trillion parameter open source model, the biggest open weight model anyone's built. Benchmarks put it close to Fable 5 and GPT-5.6 on coding and agent tasks. The catch: it already costs 3x more per task than its predecessor, and early testers are finding it slower and less reliable than the chart suggests. The real story isn't "China caught up." It's that intelligence keeps getting cheaper and more available, open or closed. That changes what you're allowed to charge for. https://platform.kimi.ai/docs/guide/kimi-k3-quickstart

  • Gemini 3.6 Flash - Google DeepMind Google shipped three efficiency model updates. Buried in the same announcement: they've started a brand new pretraining run for Gemini 4, because 3.5 Pro couldn't hit internal coding benchmarks no matter how much they tuned it. That's Google quietly admitting the current architecture hit a ceiling. Not a small thing from the company with the most compute on earth. https://www.techtimes.com/articles/321268/20260722/gemini-36-flash-cuts-token-costs-scores-higher-every-benchmark.htm

  • OpenAI Presence - OpenAI New enterprise product that plugs AI agents into a company's real systems and lets them take real actions, with human approval built into the loop. Live today, but only through OpenAI's own deployment team, not self-serve. This is OpenAI saying the model isn't the product anymore. Getting an agent to actually work inside one specific business is the product. Same conversation as the FDE episode below. https://openai.com/index/introducing-openai-presence

Enterprise AI moves

Motiv's Opal platform runs whole workflows off AI agents doing to-do-list style tasks, with humans dropped in at specific approval steps instead of full automation. The founder's pitch is basically "boring beats flashy," a plain checklist over a flowchart nobody reads.

Ushur launched an agentic platform built to finish a customer request end to end (claims, onboarding, billing) instead of the classic chatbot-then-hand-off. If you sell AI into small or mid size businesses, this is the exact gap everyone's chasing: agents that finish the job instead of agents that answer the first question and disappear.

New harnesses & tools

TinyFish's Mako is a model built just to operate the live web, reading pages the way a person does instead of scraping raw text. If you're building agents that need to use software a client already has, this is the layer that's been missing.

Perplexity shipped a GLM 5.2 based orchestrator for their Computer product. It's already the second most used model behind Opus 4.8, running at a third of the cost. Cheap orchestration is starting to matter as much as picking the flagship model.
