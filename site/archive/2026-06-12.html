LI . NO. 03
Google just showed us that language models don't have to work like typewriters.

Every text model you've ever used generates one token at a time. Left to right. No revisions once a word is placed.

DiffusionGemma works differently.

It starts with noise, like how image generators work, and refines the whole block at once. 256 tokens in parallel. Every position sees every other position at the same time.

The result: up to 4x faster on GPUs. 1,008 tokens per second on a single H100. Open source.

Most people won't feel this directly yet. But builders running local inference or low-concurrency apps will feel it first.

We're at a moment where the architecture itself is still being figured out.

The typewriter model felt permanent until it wasn't.

What else feels permanent right now that probably isn't?
